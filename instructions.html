Patient Instructions

Come Prepared, Stay Comfortable

MRI Scan

  • Arrival: Please reach 20-30 minutes early for preparation. If oral contrast is needed, arrive 45 minutes early (you will be informed during booking).
  • Pacemakers & Implants: Inform us if you have pacemakers, valves, ICDs, pumps, stents, clips, or metal implants. Some devices are not MRI-safe.
  • Claustrophobia: Our wide-bore MRI, calming lights, music, and continuous communication help reduce anxiety. A companion can stay inside for support.
  • Clothing: Wear loose and comfortable clothes. All metal items (jewellery, phone, belts, wallet, cards) must be removed.

Ultrasound

  • Drink 3-4 glasses of water 1 hour before abdominal scans.
  • No fasting needed unless told otherwise by your doctor.
  • Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing.
  • Bring all previous ultrasound reports for comparison.

Fetal Scans

  • Bring your doctor's referral and previous scan reports.
  • Best done between 12-22 weeks depending on scan type.
  • Drink water before the scan for better visualization.
  • You may bring one companion for support during the scan.

Laboratory Tests

  • Fasting Tests: 8-12 hours overnight fasting required. Water is allowed.
  • Timing: Most blood tests are done in the morning (8:30 AM - 11:00 AM).
  • Bring your doctor's prescription and ID proof.
  • Inform about any medications you're currently taking.

Nerve Conduction Studies (NCS)

  • Avoid applying creams or oils on skin on the day of test.
  • Wear loose clothing that allows access to arms and legs.
  • Inform if you have bleeding disorders or are on blood thinners.
  • The test takes 30-60 minutes depending on areas tested.

Electroencephalogram (EEG)

  • Wash and dry hair thoroughly before the test. No oils or hair products.
  • Eat normally before the test unless instructed otherwise.
  • Continue regular medications unless advised by your doctor.
  • For sleep-deprived EEG: Stay awake 4-6 hours longer than usual.

Important Notes

  • Always bring your doctor's prescription/referral letter.
  • Carry all previous medical records and scan reports.
  • Inform about allergies, medical conditions, and medications.
  • Pregnant patients must inform before any scan procedure.
  • For MRI: Remove all metallic objects including jewelry, watches, hairpins, hearing aids, and dentures.
